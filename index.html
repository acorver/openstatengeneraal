Open Staten-Generaal

Facilitating open access to and analysis of voting by Dutch parties and politicians..

Get the Data

Data Analysis and Visualization

Aggregated Data

Data has been aggregated from multiple sources, including (i) official documents from the Dutch House of Representatives ('Tweede Kamer'), (ii) webcrawlers, (iii) politicalmashup.nl

JSON

Data is stored in JSON format and can be queried using the powerful ElasticSearch API

Up-to-date

Update scripts extract the most recent votes from the website of the Dutch House of Representatives (tweedekamer.nl)

Python & GitHub

All code is written in python; it works on all platforms and is relatively easy to understand and modify. It is hosted on GitHub, so that anyone can contribute code, check revisions, submit bug reports, etc.

Political Science Tools & Techniques

Several political science algorithms are included for the analysis and visualization of the voting data

Open Source

All code is entirely open source. All contributions and feedback are greatly appreciated!

Get the Data

The entire dataset can be downloaded in JSON format here:
If you want to write your own data parsers, the ElasticSearch database dump allows you to quickly get started without having to re-run the data parsers part of this project.
Due to the hierarchical structuring of the data, it cannot usefully be made available in CSV format. We do, however, provide various useful subsets of the full dataset in CSV format.
In the future, data will be requestable through an API. Check back later.

Current Development and Future Plans

Want to help? Get in touch!

ongoing We're developing a library of algorithms that facilitate in the analysis and visualization of voting behavior.
near future We're interested in developing parsers to extract votes as far back as 1814 from archives parliamentary records.
future City council and States-Provincial votes are often available digitally on the websites of the relevant cities and provinces. Documents follow similar patterns, so it should be possible to parse the documents, make the votes digitally available, and analyze them using the algorithms already developed for the House.
future? Researching voting records of other (EU) countries? We're very interested in cooperating!

Sources

The voting record of members of and parties in the House of Representatives from 2009 onwards is crawled from the webinterface at tweedekamer.nl [1].
The voting record of members of and parties in the House of Representatives from approximately 1980 to 2013 will be supplied by the information services department of the Dutch House of Representatives [1] Data on the presence of politicians at plenary meetings of the House of Representatives can be found in the minutes of each meeting, which are available here [1] for this and last year's meetings, and here [2] for meetings older than about 4 months
